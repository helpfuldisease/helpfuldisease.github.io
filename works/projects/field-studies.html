Photography / Field observation

Field Studies Archive

A photographic notebook of terrain, plants, urban edges, light conditions, and atmospheres gathered through walking, observing, and returning to places over time.

Overview

Photography as a way of noticing landscape before designing it.

This archive sits beside the design work rather than behind it. The images record slopes, surfaces, edges, temporary weather, neglected corners, and small ecological details that can later become spatial questions.

The sequence is intentionally quiet: part travel diary, part visual research, part collection of field notes for landscape, ecology, and atmosphere.

Terrain

Topography, paths, rock, water, and seasonal ground conditions.

Edges

Urban fragments, thresholds, infrastructures, and everyday transitions.

Surfaces

Plants, bark, soil, walls, traces, and material weathering.

Atmosphere

Light, fog, exposure, color temperature, and the pace of looking.

Images

Selected field notes.

Open Terrain

Large landscape conditions, paths, slopes, and seasonal thresholds where movement makes the site readable.

Urban Edges

Everyday infrastructures and soft boundaries: streets, lights, walls, and moments where the city becomes atmospheric.

Plants And Surfaces

Close observations of vegetation, forest structure, ground texture, and the small surfaces that make a place specific.

Light And Weather

Images where atmosphere becomes the main subject: exposure, fog, snow, dimness, and the changing weight of color.
