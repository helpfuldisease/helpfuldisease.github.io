Cittaslow

Overview

Create pedestrian-friendly urban areas.

Cittaslow is a concept design for Munich's Isarvorstadt, exploring how the former Viehhof and Schlachthofviertel context can support a slower, more pedestrian-friendly neighborhood life.

The proposal rethinks street space as shared public space: a lively everyday environment for walking, cycling, sitting, temporary activities, local planting, open street culture, and informal community life.

Focus

Design focus

Open Street

Reclaiming road space for walking, cycling, lingering, and informal daily use.

Local Plants

Using planting to soften the street edge and support a richer ecological community.

Temporary Life

Allowing flexible activities to bring a rough, lively charm into the new neighborhood.

Neighborhood Furniture

Small-scale seating, tables, shade, and edges create places to pause and meet.
